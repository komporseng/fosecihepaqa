The Lyrics

The chorus of the song sets the tone, with Lil Mabu and chriseanrock proclaiming themselves as “Mr. Take Ya Bitch.” They boast about taking another man’s woman on a trip to New York and leaving their mark by signing their names on her body. The lyrics are bold and unapologetic, portraying a sense of dominance and power.

In the first verse, chrisseanrock expresses his disregard for norms and his preference for women who challenge the status quo. He asserts his independence and willingness to do whatever it takes to satisfy his desires, even if it means breaking societal expectations. The mention of water breaking symbolizes the beginning of a new life, further emphasizing his rebellious nature.

In the second verse, Lil Mabu takes aim at Wack 100, a controversial figure in the music industry known for his association with snitches. Lil Mabu confronts him directly, insinuating that Wack 100 is racist and suggesting that he is ready to defend himself with force. The use of violence and aggression in his lyrics reflects the intensity and passion behind his words.

The song quickly gained attention on social media platforms, with many expressing their disdain for its explicit content and derogatory language. Critics labeled it as the “worst song of the century,” highlighting the contentious nature of its lyrics.

However, amidst the controversy, rumors of a romantic relationship between Lil Mabu and chriseanrock began to circulate. Some fans speculated that the intense chemistry displayed in the song extended beyond the recording studio. While the artists themselves have not confirmed or denied these rumors, it adds an extra layer of intrigue to the track.
