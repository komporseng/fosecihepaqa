William Daniels Biography

William Daniels is an American actor who was born and brought up as William David Daniels in Brooklyn, New York City, New York. He is popularly known for his television roles, notably as Mark Craig in the drama series St. Elsewhere, for which he won two Primetime Emmy Awards; the voice of KITT in the television series Knight Rider and as George Feeny in the sitcom Boy Meets World, which earned him four People’s Choice Award nominations.

    William Daniels Career

    In the 1950s, Daniels started his career in the entertainment industry as a stage actor. He made his Broadway debut in the year 1952 in the play Life with Father and went on to appear in a couple of other stage productions, such as 1776, On Golden Pond and The Lion in Winter. He started appearing in television and film, with his first film role coming in the 1967 film The Graduate. He has appeared in a couple of television series, such as St. Elsewhere, The Larry Sanders Show and Boy Meets World. He has also worked as a voice actor, providing the voice for KITT in the television series Knight Rider and voicing many characters in the animated series The Magic School Bus.
