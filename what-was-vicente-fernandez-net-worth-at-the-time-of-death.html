Vicente Fernández Gómez was a Mexican musician, songwriter, actor, and film producer who lived from 17 February 1940 until 12 December 2021. Vicente is known by the moniker “Chente,” which is short for Vicente, as well as “El Charro de Huentitán,” “El dolo de México,” and “El Rey de la Msica Ranchera,” After beginning his career as a busker and recording more than 100 albums and appearing in more than 30 films, Fernández went on to become a cultural legend. He played rancheras and other traditional Mexican music.

Fernández received four Grammy Awards, nine Latin Grammy Awards, fourteen Lo Nuestro Awards, and a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame for his work. He sold more than 50 million records worldwide, making him one of the most successful regional Mexican artists of all time.

Fernández stopped performing live in 2016, but he continued to record and publish music. With their “200 Best Singers of All Time” list in 2023, Rolling Stone named Fernández the greatest Mexican singer of all time and the 95th greatest overall.
